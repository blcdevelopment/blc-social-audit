What this section tells you

Search Console shows how people already find the site in Google. It highlights pages with visibility but weak clicks, search terms close to stronger rankings, and pages losing traffic. These numbers come directly from Google, not from estimates.

How to read the columns

Impressions = how many times the page appeared in Google results. Clicks = how many times searchers chose it. CTR (click-through rate) = clicks divided by impressions; high impressions with low CTR means the page is seen but not chosen. Position = the average ranking spot in the results (1 is the top).

Context for a local business

Where your leads really come from

The local pack is your #1 lead driver

For a local service business, the Google Map pack and your Google Business Profile drive more calls and direction requests than classic blue-link rankings — local searchers click the map results more often than the standard listings. This audit checks the on-page signals that support local ranking (business-identity and address structured data, a visible address, a Maps/Profile link, and service-area markup — see the SEO findings). It cannot see your Business Profile itself, so the next step is to claim and complete it, gather reviews, and keep your name, address, and phone identical everywhere.

AI Overviews — and why your money searches are more insulated

Google's AI Overviews are reducing clicks, but mostly on broad informational searches where a summary answers the question. Your highest-value searches are local and high-intent ("builder near me", "kitchen remodel [city]") and the Map pack — these are far less substitutable by a summary. The defensive plays are the same ones that win locally: own the local pack and Business Profile, earn reviews, and give clear, well-structured answers (headings, FAQs, and schema) so answer engines can cite you.
